Police Killings in USA in 2015 Grouped by Races

There are lots of police killing cases that happen in USA in 2015. Moreover, race and ethnicity are prejudiced as one of the factor that increases police killing cases. This visualisation will briefly show what correlation that race and ethnicity have with the other trend that could possibly affect police killing.

Police Killings Grouped by Race/Ethnicity in Different States

From the map, when we filter through the legends, the victims was mostly White Americans followed by Black Americans, Latino Americans, Asian Americans, and lastly Native Americans. Meanwhile, when filtering by states, California accounts for the highest police killings with total of 74 cases.When we brush through the line chart, the month with the most police killing cases during 2015 year is March with 114 cases.

Correlation Between Race/Ethnicity and Age of Victims

When we brush through the line chart, we can see the distribution of victim's age through different races. Mostly the victim's age ranging from 28 to 40 with the peak 18 records of victim aged 29 and 39. In addition, most of the victims on the range is White Americans followed by Black Americans as well.

Cause Of Death based on Races

Armed or Not ?

The most common cause of death are gunshots for all social group based on race/ethnicity with more than 400 records. It is relatively huge compared to the other cause of death. unclear US gun policy which allows individuals to carry gun in public without any certificate could become one of the main reason of the high police killing cases that happen in USA. Most of the victims are armed whether with firearm , knife, or even non lethal firearm. 45 % of White Americans were carrying firearms and Asian Americans accounted for the lowest rate of victim with firearm. Surprisingly, the rate of unarmed victims are quite high which is unusual.

Conclusion

Throughout the dashboard we can see that White Americans with age ranging from 28 to 40 are mostly killed across USA followed by Black Americans. Based on the states, California is the states with most police killing cases where Alaska and Maine accounts for the states with lowest police killing cases. Race and Ethnicity is not the only factor that contribute to police killing but There are also other factor such as age, gender, and whether they are armed or not that could increase the rate of police killings.
